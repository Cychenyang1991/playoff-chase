From Overlooked Second-Round Pick to King of New York

  • Jalen Brunson was drafted 33rd overall in the 2018 NBA Draft — a second-round pick. Seven years later, he owns the most 30-point playoff games in New York Knicks franchise history.
  • In Game 1 of the Eastern Conference Semifinals against Philadelphia, Brunson erupted: 27 points in the first half alone, finishing with 35 as the Knicks rolled to a 39-point victory (137–98).
  • In Game 3 in Philadelphia, with the 76ers desperate to avoid a 3-0 hole, Brunson delivered 33 points and 9 assists, burying a dagger three late in the fourth to silence the crowd.
  • He has scored at least 15 in all 49 Knicks playoff games — a streak mentioned alongside Michael Jordan (179 straight with Chicago) and Kareem Abdul-Jabbar (57 straight with Milwaukee) for the longest 15-point opening playoff streaks with one team.
  • The Knicks have won six consecutive playoff games, five by double digits. They are the first team in NBA playoff history to win three straight games by 25+ points.
  • Karl-Anthony Towns has been the perfect co-star: the Brunson–Towns duo has matched Frazier and DeBusschere (1970s title era) for the most 20+ point playoff games by a duo in franchise history.
  • With OG Anunoby sidelined by a hamstring injury, Brunson has elevated further — proof that New York's title hopes run through the point guard.
  • One more win vs. Philadelphia sends the Knicks back to the Eastern Conference Finals for a second straight year — with eyes on the franchise's first NBA Finals since 1999.
