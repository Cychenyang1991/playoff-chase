From Villain to Hero in 48 Hours

  • In Game 1 on May 5, Austin Reaves produced what ESPN called the worst shooting performance by a Laker in a playoff game in 35 years. His 18.8% field goal percentage on 16 attempts was historically bad.
  • Reaves had only recently returned from a Grade 2 left oblique strain suffered on April 2 against the same Thunder team. Game 1 was just his third game back from a month-long absence.
  • The criticism was immediate and brutal. NBA Twitter and sports media eviscerated the undrafted guard who had averaged 23.3 PPG during the regular season.
  • LeBron James defended him: "He was out a month... his presence alone helps us, no matter what." Coach JJ Redick was equally supportive: "He's a great player. He's gonna bounce back."
  • In Game 2 on May 7, Reaves responded with the best playoff performance of his career: 31 points on sizzling 62.5% shooting and 50% from three.
  • He broke a streak of 14 consecutive missed three-pointers dating back to the first round.
  • The Lakers still lost 125-107 to fall behind 0-2 in the series, but Reaves' resurgence gave the team hope heading back to Los Angeles for Game 3.
  • Without Luka Dončić (hamstring injury, expected out 3 more weeks), Reaves' return to form is the Lakers' biggest — and perhaps only — silver lining.

What's at Stake

  • Series status: Lakers trail Thunder 0-2 heading into Game 3 in Los Angeles (May 10).
  • Luka Dončić: Out indefinitely with a strained left hamstring, unlikely to return for the semifinals.
  • Reaves' contract: The 27-year-old is due for a new contract this summer that would solidify his status as one of the sport's star players.
  • The bottom line: If Reaves can sustain his Game 2 production at home, the Lakers have a fighting chance to tie the series. If the Game 1 version returns, a sweep is inevitable.
