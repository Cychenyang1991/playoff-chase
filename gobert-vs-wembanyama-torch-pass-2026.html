The Prodigy and the Master: Gobert's Darkest Hour

Rudy Gobert is a 4-time Defensive Player of the Year. He just held Nikola Jokić — the best offensive center alive — below his season averages in the first round. He has been Victor Wembanyama's mentor, friend, and French national team brother for nearly a decade. And now, his 22-year-old protégé is systematically dismantling him on the biggest stage in basketball. 39 points on 72% shooting in Game 3. A Hakeem Olajuwon spin fadeaway taught by the legend himself — executed over Gobert. A unanimous DPOY trophy that once felt like a coronation now hanging over one man's twilight and another's dawn. This is the story of the most personal passing of the torch in NBA playoff history.

Milestone Progress

More than 90% of Game 3's spotlight — Gobert couldn't slow the storm

The 18-point 4Q

Wembanyama's fourth quarter landed like a statement on a four-time DPOY: 16 points in the final frame, three Olajuwon spin fadeaways over Gobert, a 25-foot dagger minutes later, a dime mixed in — 18 of 29 Spurs fourth-quarter points created.

Minnesota shot 4-for-21 (19%) when directly challenged by Wembanyama at the rim — while Gobert mustered 3-6 shooting and 7 boards. The two-way ledger tilted hard.

The Hakeem Olajuwon spin fadeaway — three times over Gobert

Dream's lessons

In the fourth alone, Wembanyama unleashed the Dream spin fade three times — each over Gobert.

Wembanyama: I had to resort to some things that Hakeem taught me in the fourth quarter. Many things. Especially that spin fadeaway over Rudy.

Last summer with Olajuwon; on Thursday night, the full syllabus against the most decorated defensive big alive — his countryman and mentor. ESPN called one look a shot right out of Olajuwon's arsenal: a spinning fadeaway.

Gobert has seen that footwork on film for 12 seasons. Recognition didn't equal a stop — the student had passed the lesson in real time.

The DPOY torch pass — from 4-time winner to unanimous phenom

The torch pass

Gobert shares the all-time mark at four DPOYs. Wembanyama just became the first unanimous winner — and the youngest ever.

Gobert finished 4th in 2025-26 voting; Wembanyama swept 100 first-place votes while leading San Antonio to 62 wins with ~25 PPG and a league-high 3.1 blocks.

Voters still respect Gobert's rim protection — he held Jokić under season norms in round one — but the narrative has shifted to Wembanyama's generational footprint.

Rookie-year Wembanyama: Let him win it now, because after that, it's no longer his turn. He kept the promise.

Gobert to ESPN: I'm proud and very happy for him — adding that Wembanyama may someday pass his four DPOYs.

Brothers in arms

Outside of the talent, he's someone that has a very unique soul, very unique mind and nothing is an accident. Nobody his age ever had this kind of maturity.

Gobert on their talks: We have so many conversations about how to maximize your physical potential, but it's much deeper than that. It's (about) how to maximize our spiritual and mental potential. Wembanyama's questions go granular — even water filters.

Game 1's 12 blocks — the teacher's prophecy

The teacher's prophecy

Game 1: Wembanyama's 12 blocks — new playoff record, snapping the shared 10 of Eaton, Olajuwon, Bynum — plus the third blocks triple-double in postseason history.

Gobert before the playoffs: I'm proud and very happy for him. He will be Defensive Player of the Year. And he'll probably get it unanimous. Wembanyama broke the record in Game 1, swept the DPOY vote, then detonated for 39 on Gobert in Game 3.

Gobert has never blocked 12 in a playoff game — his protégé did it in his sixth career postseason outing.

From Mentorship to Playoff Rivalry

  • Two French towers bonded through national team duty despite a decade between NBA arrivals. Camp in 2017: Wembanyama 13, already 5-11; Gobert a rising NBA name and curiosity curator.
  • Three years on, Gobert sought workouts with the phenom — who had sprouted past him in height. After that, we became friends, and I was paying very close attention to his development, he told The Athletic.
  • The talks ran deeper than footwork: body, mind, spirit. Wembanyama interrogated every detail — filters, recovery, routines.
  • Rookie bravado: Let him win it now, because after that, it's no longer his turn. April 2026: first unanimous DPOY, youngest recipient.
  • First playoff meeting: Gobert 33, best years in the rearview; Wembanyama 22, authoring the loudest two-way night vs. a 4× DPOY in the modern game. The box score screams; the postgame chess still whispers.
  • More than a series — a generational handoff in French basketball. Gobert defined the modern rim. Wembanyama is redefining it — sometimes on Gobert's head.
