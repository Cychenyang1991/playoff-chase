How Holmgren Is Redefining Playoff Defense

  • At 7-foot-1 with a 7-foot-6 wingspan, Holmgren combines elite rim protection with the lateral quickness to switch onto guards. The Lakers have tried targeting him in pick-and-roll — it's not working.
  • In Game 2, with SGA in foul trouble in the third quarter, Holmgren took over on both ends. He scored or assisted on 12 of OKC's 18 points during a pivotal 18-8 run, while simultaneously anchoring the defense that held the Lakers to 28.6% shooting in the quarter.
  • Through two games, the Lakers are averaging just 95.5 points — 15.6 points below their regular season average. Holmgren's presence is the single biggest factor.
  • His 4 steals in Game 2 were a playoff career high, showcasing his ability to defend in space and jump passing lanes.
  • The "Holmgren Effect": Lakers players are passing out of the paint 31.4% of the time when Holmgren is in position — well above the league average of 22.1%. He doesn't even need to block shots to change them.
  • Only three players in NBA history have averaged 2+ blocks and shot 40%+ from three in a single postseason: Holmgren is on pace to be the fourth — and the youngest.
