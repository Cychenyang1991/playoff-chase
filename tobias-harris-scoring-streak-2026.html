Redemption Arc

From Regular Season Role Player to Playoff Hero

  • During the 2025-26 regular season, Tobias Harris averaged just 13.3 points per game and never once scored 20+ points in consecutive games across 63 appearances.
  • In the playoffs, he has transformed into Detroit's second scoring option, averaging 22.7 PPG over his last seven games — a 71% increase.
  • This is the longest 20-point playoff streak in NBA history by a player who didn't record a single back-to-back 20-point game in the preceding regular season.
  • Harris scored 75 total points across the three elimination games (Games 5-7) against Orlando, including a playoff career-high 30 in Game 7 with five three-pointers.
  • Pistons head coach J.B. Bickerstaff called Harris "our safety blanket" and said: "Without him, we wouldn't be in this position. Not just because of the basketball talent — his leadership, his willingness to show people what preparation looks like."
  • Cade Cunningham called Harris "a pro's pro" and said: "We're lucky to have him. He set the tone early in Game 7 and was a huge reason why we won."
  • Harris and Cunningham have joined LeBron James & Dwyane Wade, and Shaq & Kobe, as the only duos in NBA history to each score 20+ points in 5 consecutive playoff wins.
  • Harris overcame a first-half hip injury in Game 2 vs Cleveland to finish with 21 points, 7 rebounds, 2 steals, and 2 blocks — the defensive effort sealing Detroit's win.

The 15-Year Journey

  • 15 NBA seasons across 5 teams (Bucks, Magic, Pistons, Clippers, 76ers, Pistons again)
  • 1,033+ regular season games played
  • 67+ playoff games played across 8 playoff series before 2026
  • Career regular season averages: 15.9 PPG, 6.1 RPG, 2.4 APG
  • Career playoff averages (before 2026): 16.3 PPG, 8.2 RPG, 3.0 APG
  • 2011: Drafted 19th overall by Charlotte, traded to Milwaukee
  • 2016: First playoff appearance with Detroit (swept by Cavaliers in Round 1)
  • 2019: Signed 5-year, $180 million contract with Philadelphia
  • 2023-24: Pistons won just 14 games
  • 2024: Signed 2-year, $52 million deal to return to Detroit
  • 2025-26: Pistons win 60 games (most since 2005-06), clinch #1 seed in East
