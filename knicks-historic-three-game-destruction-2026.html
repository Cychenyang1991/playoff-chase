Milestone Progress

First team ever to win three straight playoff games by 25+ points

NBA history

The Knicks are the first team in NBA history to win three consecutive playoff games by 25 points or more. No team — not the 1996 Bulls, not the 2017 Warriors, not the 1986 Celtics — had done it before.

The streak: +29 vs. Hawks (G5), +51 vs. Hawks (G6), +39 vs. 76ers (G1).

The NBA has staged the playoffs since 1947. In 79 years of postseason basketball, nobody had strung together three such blowouts — until this Knicks run.

How the Knicks Became a Playoff Wrecking Ball

  • The 2026 New York Knicks are doing something no team in NBA history has done: stacking 25+ point playoff victories, night after night after night.
  • In Game 5 against Atlanta, Jalen Brunson dropped 27 points and the Knicks won by 29 — a statement that the Hawks' season was living on borrowed time.
  • In Game 6 on the road, the Knicks delivered the most devastating postseason beatdown in franchise history: a 47-point halftime lead (an NBA playoff record), a 51-point final margin (tied for 6th-largest in playoff history), and 140 points (a Knicks playoff high). Atlanta was eliminated in humiliating fashion.
  • In Game 1 against Philadelphia, the foot never left the gas. Brunson erupted for 27 first-half points; the team shot 63%; the final was 137-98 — a 39-point rout of a 76ers club that had just survived seven games against Boston.
  • The math: three games, three wins, +119 in the margin column. No team in 79 years of NBA playoffs has authored a more dominant three-game stretch.
  • New York has held opponents under 100 in 5 of 9 playoff games. Defensive rating 103.9 ranks 2nd; offensive rating 122.0 also ranks 2nd. This is a complete, two-way machine.
  • Karl-Anthony Towns' defensive leap matters: the Knicks' defensive rating with him on the floor in the playoffs is 10.6 points per 100 better than the regular season.
  • "We've got to be the aggressor," Brunson said after Game 3 in Philadelphia. The Knicks have lived that mantra — and the record books are bending because of it.
