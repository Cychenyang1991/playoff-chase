The Hero and the Unicorn Wall: Edwards vs. Wembanyama

Anthony Edwards hyperextended his left knee and suffered a bone bruise on April 25. Doctors expected him to miss weeks — including the first two games of this series. Nine days later, he was back on the floor. He scored 18 points off the bench to steal Game 1. He poured in 32 points and 14 rebounds in Game 3 while gutting through 40.5 minutes on an injured leg. And it still wasn't enough. Because on the other side stood Victor Wembanyama — the unanimous Defensive Player of the Year who blocked 12 shots in Game 1, scored 39 in Game 3, and built a wall that even a superhero couldn't break.

Milestone Progress

The 9-day miracle — returned weeks ahead of schedule

9-day miracle

Edwards played 9 days after a left knee hyperextension and bone bruise in Game 4 of round one vs. Denver on April 25, 2026.

He landed awkwardly contesting a layup, was helped off, and missed the Nuggets series' final two games.

Original timeline: Multiple weeks; most insiders ruled him out for Game 1.

May 5: Upgraded to questionable after shootaround, cleared after pre-game work, came off the bench with a minutes limit.

Mike Conley: Nobody expected him to play. It was just his level of commitment to the game. Not just to the game, but to his teammates. It showed a lot.

Game 1 — the hero off the bench (18 PTS in 25 MIN)

Bench hero

18 points in 25 bench minutes — including 11 in the fourth that helped turn a 3-point deficit into a 95-86 lead. Wolves 104-102, home court stolen.

Sequence: 11 of Minnesota's first 19 fourth-quarter points; a 7-0 surge for control.

Minutes limit, first playoff bench role — still outshone a historic 12-block night from Wembanyama.

Edwards: I know for a fact, just me being out there, it calms everybody down. Not saying there's any pressure on any of my teammates, but it takes pressure off of everybody just knowing that I'm out there, I'm available to play.

Game 3 — full warrior mode (32 PTS, 14 REB, 6 AST, 40.5 MIN)

Warrior mode

32 PTS, 14 REB (tying his playoff career high), 6 AST, 1 BLK, 3 threes on 12-of-26 in 40:30 — on a bad leg, dragging Minnesota from an 18-3 hole and a 15-point third-quarter deficit. Within 5 (107-102) before Wembanyama closed.

Historic note: It was Edwards' sixth 30-point playoff game in the last 363 days, putting him on an all-time list ahead of Michael Jordan for most 30-point playoff games in a single postseason.

Chip Scoggins (Star Tribune): Edwards was fabulous in gutting through 40½ minutes on an injured leg, posting 32 points, 14 rebounds and six assists. Victor Wembanyama was even better.

Edwards: If we make our shots, we win this game.

The 12-block wall — NBA playoff record

NBA history

12 blocks in Game 1 — the NBA playoff single-game record.

Previous high (10): Mark Eaton (1985), Hakeem Olajuwon (1990), Andrew Bynum (2012).

Triple-double: 11 PTS, 15 REB, 12 BLK — third player since 1973-74 with a blocks triple-double in the playoffs.

Start: 6 blocks in the first 14 minutes; 11 before the third ended.

At the rim vs. Wembanyama: Wolves 4-for-21 (19%) when directly challenged.

Game 3 — the 39-point answer (35/15/5 club)

Hall of Fame club

39 PTS, 15 REB, 5 BLK on 13-of-18 (72.2%) — joined Kareem, Hakeem, and Shaq as the only playoff 35/15/5 games ever.

First ever at that line on 70%+ from the field.

Fourth: 16 points after his 5th foul (6:18 left); 25-foot dagger at 3:06; created 18 of 29 Spurs fourth-quarter points (score + assist).

Hakeem spin fadeaway over Rudy Gobert — French national-team mentor, 4-time DPOY — using footwork Olajuwon installed last summer.

The Wembanyama effect — deterrence beyond blocks

The Wembanyama effect

Minnesota has had to re-architect its attack around his shadow.

Game 1 at rim vs. Wemby: 4-for-21 (19%). Game 3: McDaniels + Randle 8-for-34 (23.5%) combined.

Tactics tried: Pick-and-roll to stress him in space, Gobert screens to bump him from the rim, attacking closeouts — nothing has stuck consistently.

Ripple: Extra passes out of the paint, altered arcs, hesitated drives — even when he isn't the help defender.

The ice on both knees — the toll of shouldering everything

The toll

After Game 2's 133-95 loss, Edwards left with ice on both knees — an image that went viral.

Through three games he has been Minnesota's steadiest creator. Donte DiVincenzo remains out (right Achilles); the rest of the rotation has wobbled vs. San Antonio's defense.

Reality: Even superhero shot-making has a ceiling when the league's best rim eraser owns the paint.

Edwards: I went in the locker room and told the guys we had a bunch of great looks. If we make our shots, we win this game.

Nine Days from Disaster to Heroism

  • April 25: Edwards hit the deck in Game 4 vs. Denver — hyperextended knee, bone bruise. He sat the final two games as Minnesota closed the Nuggets. Timeline: weeks. The Spurs series looked as if it might open without him.
  • Nine days later he was active: questionable after shootaround, cleared after workouts, bench role in Game 1. The player carried off the court checked in and changed the temperature.
  • Fourth quarter, Game 1: 11 of the Wolves' first 19 points in the period; 3-point hole to 95-86. Final 104-102. Wembanyama's 12 blocks entered the record book — Edwards still walked out a winner.
  • Game 2: hobbled, overwhelmed; 133-95 — largest postseason loss in franchise history. Dual knee ice packs postgame.
  • Game 3: 32 / 14 / 6 in 40.5 minutes; climbed out of 18-3; within 5 late. Best non-Wembanyama player on the floor.
  • Not enough: Wembanyama 39 on 72%, 16 in the fourth on 5 fouls, Olajuwon package vs. Gobert, 25-foot dagger — he matched the gutsiest Edwards night and raised it.
  • Wolves trail 2-1. Edwards is running on fumes. The Spurs defense — built around the best rim protector alive — has turned the offense into attrition. The hero has emptied the clip. The wall still stands.

Game 4: The Hero's Last Stand?

  • Game 4: Target Center, Minneapolis — Sunday May 10 / Monday May 11 (confirm schedule), 7:30 PM ET, NBC / Peacock.
  • Series: Spurs 2-1. A San Antonio win in Game 4 means 3-1 before a San Antonio Game 5.
  • Edwards' health: Three games in five days on a knee that was supposed to need weeks — post-Game 2 dual ice went viral. How long can he sustain this level?
  • Support: DiVincenzo still out (Achilles). Naz Reid (18 / 9 in Game 3) and Gobert (13 in Game 3) have been up and down. Randle + McDaniels 8-for-34 in Game 3.
  • The Wembanyama problem: ~23 PPG, 15 RPG, 5.7 BPG through three games; Game 3 with Kareem/Hakeem/Shaq company; Game 1 record blocks. The Unicorn Wall is the schematic and emotional reality.
  • Narrative: Steal Game 4 to 2-2 and the series becomes an instant classic; if the wall holds, a 3-1 hole that almost no one escapes.
  • Legacy: Win or lose, the 9-day comeback and 40-minute Game 3 burnish Edwards as one of the league's toughest stars — but the scoreboard pays in wins, and the wall is ahead on points.
