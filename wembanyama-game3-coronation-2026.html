The Hakeem spin fadeaway — over Gobert

Master's move

Late, with the game in the balance, Wembanyama caught the post against 4-time DPOY Rudy Gobert — his French national-team mentor — spun, faded from 15 feet, and swished. The package came straight from Hakeem Olajuwon, who trained Wembanyama the prior summer.

Wembanyama: I had to resort to some things that Hakeem taught me in the fourth quarter. Many things. Especially that spin fadeaway over Rudy.

A 22-year-old deploying a Hall of Famer's footwork to score on a four-time DPOY — and elder statesman of the same national program — in the biggest game of his life.

How Game 3 Became Wemby's Arrival Statement

  • Spurs and Wolves were tied 1-1 entering Game 3 at Target Center. Game 1: a Wembanyama defensive epic (12 blocks) spoiled by offense (5-for-17). Game 2: a 38-point San Antonio rout. Game 3 would tilt the series narrative.
  • Wembanyama opened with purpose: the Spurs' first seven points, including a reverse alley-oop that hushed the building. An 18-3 start. Minnesota rallied behind Anthony Edwards (22 first-half points) to tie 51-51 at halftime.
  • The third quarter stretched the lead behind 6-for-10 team threes — but the Wolves kept clawing. With 6:18 left in the fourth and a 99-98 edge, Jaden McDaniels drew Wembanyama's 5th foul. One whistle from disqualification.
  • Mitch Johnson's gamble: roughly 60 seconds on the bench, then back in with five personals. We were going to not leave any bullets in the chamber in terms of keeping him on the bench, Johnson said. If he fouled out, we deal with that.
  • What followed: 16 points in the final 7 minutes — the Olajuwon spin over Gobert, the 25-foot dagger at 3:06, the defensive board on the next possession.
  • Final line: 39 on 13-of-18 (72.2%), 15 REB, 5 BLK, 1 TO — fourth member of the playoff 35/15/5 club, at an efficiency none of the others matched.
  • They're Wolves, after all, Wembanyama grinned postgame. It's going to happen. Then: It's good to be along with the big fellas.
  • Stephon Castle: He was everywhere. Without him, I don't know if we win today. That's his character. On the road, tied series. We had them on their heels the whole game.
