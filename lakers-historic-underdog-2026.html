Milestone Progress Bars — Climbing the Mountain

Milestone 1: The +950 Series Price — Lakers' Biggest Playoff Underdog Since 1988

The Lakers are +950 underdogs to win this series, the largest playoff underdog price for the franchise since at least 1988 — as far back as the Sports Odds History database tracks. The previous franchise high was +650 against the San Antonio Spurs in 2013. The Lakers were also +550 underdogs against Houston in Round 1 earlier this postseason. LeBron's career context: only once has he been a bigger series underdog — +1200 with the 2006 Cavaliers against the Detroit Pistons (lost 4-2). Other comparable spots: +690 vs. Warriors in 2018 (swept 4-0), +600 vs. Celtics in 2008 (lost 4-3).

Milestone 4: The Historical Precedent — 2-26 When 13+ Point Underdogs in Game 1

Since the NBA merger, teams that were 13-point underdogs or more in Game 1 of a playoff series are just 2-26 straight up in that opening game. The only two outright wins: 1993 Lakers against Phoenix, and 1989 Warriors against Utah — both considered major outliers. Against the spread, those teams went 11-16-1 ATS in Game 1 — competitive but below expectations. Series outcome: of those 28 teams, only ONE went on to win the series — the 1989 Warriors. Every other team lost the series.

Milestone 7: The 1989 Warriors — The Only Team That Did It

Only ONE team in NBA history has won a playoff series after being 13+ point underdogs in Game 1 — the 1989 Golden State Warriors, who upset the Utah Jazz. The 1989 Warriors: led by Chris Mullin, they were a 7-seed that swept the 2-seed Jazz 3-0 in the first round — one of the most shocking upsets in NBA history. The 1993 Lakers: the only other team to win Game 1 as a 13+ point dog — they beat the Charles Barkley-led Suns in Game 1 but lost the series in five. For the 2026 Lakers to win this series, they would need to accomplish something only ONE team has done in nearly 50 years of NBA playoff history.

By the Numbers: How Big Is This Underdog Gap?

  • The Lakers' +950 series price is the largest conference semifinal underdog position for the franchise since 2007, when they were 10-1 against the Phoenix Suns.
  • LeBron James has only entered a playoff series at a longer price once — +1200 against the 2006 Detroit Pistons (lost 4-2). At +690 against the 2018 Warriors, he was swept. At +600 against the 2008 Celtics, he lost in seven.
  • The common thread is clear: when LeBron has been priced this far out, the opponent has almost always been at or near a championship level. This Thunder team fits that description as the defending NBA champion.
  • The Thunder opened at -1600 to win the series on DraftKings Sportsbook, with the most likely outcome being a sweep (+120 for Thunder 4-0).
  • After two games, the Thunder's implied win probability has risen to 99.8% according to betting markets — meaning sportsbooks consider the series essentially over.
  • Despite all of this, 97% of the betting money at BetMGM is on the Lakers to win the series. The public is betting with their hearts, not the odds.
  • The Lakers' last 5-game ATS record as an underdog of 8.5 points or more: 1-4. They haven't beaten this kind of expectation all season.

Why the Lakers Believe

  • The Lakers already defied the odds once this postseason. They entered Round 1 as +425 underdogs against the Houston Rockets — and won the series 4-2 despite being without Luka Dončić for all six games and Austin Reaves for portions.
  • Kevin Durant missed five of the six games for Houston, evening the playing field. But the Lakers' defense — which held the Rockets under 100 points in four of six games — was legitimate.
  • LeBron James is 41 years old and playing in his 300th career playoff game. He has seen every possible playoff scenario. He knows what it takes to shock the world.
  • Austin Reaves' Game 2 resurgence (31 points, playoff career high) after his historically bad Game 1 (8 points on 18.8% shooting) proves that bounce-backs happen — and that the Lakers' spirit is unbroken.
  • The series shifts to Los Angeles for Games 3 and 4. The Crypto.com Arena crowd — one of the most intimidating in sports — could be the equalizer that evens this series.
  • Luka Dončić's potential return hangs over the series. His Grade 2 hamstring strain has an 8-week timeline from April 2 — putting a potential Game 7 return in play. If he comes back, all bets are off.
